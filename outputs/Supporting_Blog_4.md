## Introduction

Root canal treatment is a common dental procedure used to save infected or damaged teeth. Modern techniques make the procedure safe and effective.

## What Is Root Canal Treatment?

A root canal removes infected pulp from inside the tooth, cleans the canals, and seals the tooth to prevent future infection.

## Procedure Steps

### Diagnosis

The dentist examines the tooth and may take X-rays.

### Cleaning

The infected pulp is removed from the tooth.

### Filling

The cleaned canals are filled and sealed.

### Restoration

A crown may be placed to strengthen the tooth.

## Benefits of Root Canal Treatment

- Saves natural teeth
- Relieves pain
- Prevents infection spread
- Restores normal chewing function

## Recovery

Most patients return to normal activities within a short period. Following the dentist's instructions helps ensure proper healing.

## Cost Factors

- Tooth location
- Severity of infection
- Need for additional restoration
- Dentist's expertise

## FAQs

### Is root canal treatment painful?
Modern root canal procedures are generally comfortable due to local anesthesia.

### How long does recovery take?
Most patients recover within a few days.

### Will I need a crown afterward?
Many treated teeth benefit from a crown for added protection.

## Conclusion

Root canal treatment is an effective way to preserve natural teeth and maintain oral health. Early treatment can prevent more serious dental problems.
